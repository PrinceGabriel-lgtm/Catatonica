\## What Catatonica Is



Catatonica is not a meditation app. It is not a timer. It is not a productivity tool, though it can make you more productive. It is not a wellness product, though it can make you well.



Catatonica is a \*\*pause between punches.\*\*



In boxing, in MMA, there is a moment between throwing the punch and the next action. It happens in milliseconds. But it decides what the next two actions will be. Sometimes taking the moment to analyze your opponent allows you to predict the next ten moves accordingly. Foot placement matters — yours and the opponent's. Breath matters. Timing matters. Eyes matter.



That millisecond is the product.



The opponent isn't another person. It is the situation. The pressure. The decision you can't make. The grief. The thing you're carrying. Catatonica trains you to take the millisecond. Not to avoid the moment — to \*use\* it.



This is not about doing nothing. It is about learning the second before the next move.

\## The Practice



The practice has three pieces. Each is a name for something that was already happening. The naming is what makes it a practice.



\### Situations



A situation is what you are carrying.



A decision you cannot make. A transition you did not ask for. A pressure that will not lift. A grief that is taking longer than you thought it would. A relationship that is changing shape. A project that is not working.



You name it. You do not solve it. The act of naming is the first act of the practice. You are telling yourself: \*yes, I am carrying this. I see it. I am not pretending I am not.\*



You can carry many situations at once. Each one keeps its own record.



\### Sessions



A session is a time you choose to sit.



Not to fix anything. Not to meditate in any particular way. Just to sit with what you are carrying, for a period of time, inside one of three states.



The states are not cosmology. They are practical:



\*\*Breath.\*\* Teaches you to breathe again. For when you have forgotten. For when your chest is tight and your shoulders have been holding something for hours without you noticing. A single slow-breathing circle on screen. A gentle ambient. Your job is to follow, not to think.



\*\*Anchor.\*\* Soft sound to catch attention when it drifts. No visuals. Open your eyes to the room. Close them when you're ready. The sound is not music. It is a hand on your shoulder. It says: \*here, come back to here, it is okay.\*



\*\*Silence after the hit.\*\* Modeled after the exact moment after a strike in boxing. That \*shhhhh.\* Not silence as absence — silence as recovery. Silence as the second you need to decide whether you are standing up before the count ends. Dead calm. Dead still. No timer visible. No animation. You can stay eight seconds or twenty minutes. You will know when you are ready to stand.



\### Catatons



A Cataton is one minute of earned stillness.



Like a photon carries light, a Cataton carries potential. You do not spend it. You do not trade it. It is the record that you sat. It accumulates for the rest of your life and it never resets.



Some days you earn one. Some days you earn thirty. The Cataton is not a score. It is a particle — the smallest measurable unit of the practice.



Over time, Catatons compose stages. The stages are markers of how long you have been practicing, not of how well. They unlock additional states when you are ready for them. You decide when you are ready. The system only tells you when you \*can\* step up. You choose whether to.



| Stage | Earned at | Available states |

|---|---|---|

| Acknowledgement | 0 Catatons | Breath |

| Acceptance | 5 Catatons | + Anchor |

| Receiving | 15 Catatons | + Silence after the hit |

| The Threshold | 40 Catatons | All states + ability to extend or customize |



Stepping up is a ritual, not an upgrade. It happens between sessions. One confirmation. One acceptance. The stage changes. You can step back down whenever you want. The practice is not linear. Sometimes you need the simpler state again.



\### Planned Obsolescence



Nothing you carry is meant to be carried forever.



Every situation has a natural lifespan — a point where the work is done and the thing no longer needs to be held. \*You\* decide when that is. Not when the pressure lifts. Not when the answer arrives. When you are ready.



When you release a situation, you write what you are letting go. The text dissolves. Your Catatons from that situation crystallize and move to your permanent record. The situation moves to the Chronicle as past tense. A new chapter opens.



This is the most important feature of the product. It is the thing no other app in the category has. Everyone teaches you how to start. Catatonica teaches you how to finish.



\### The Chronicle



The Chronicle is a journal that writes itself.



You do not log. You do not tag. Every session you do, every situation you name, every release you perform — it is there. Grouped by situation. Timestamped. Quiet.



Six months in, you scroll back and find you sat with something on March 3rd. You barely remember the day. But you sat with it. And now you are on the other side of it. The Chronicle is the proof that you survived yourself.

\## How Catatonica Lives in a Day



Catatonica is not a destination. It is a companion that runs alongside your life.



There are three moments when you reach for it:



\### The Waking Practice



Deliberate, during-the-day use. You are at your desk. You pause. You take five minutes. You return to work. This is the default experience. Timer-bounded. Mode-selectable. Chronicle logs it.



This is the practice as exercise — regular, chosen, built into the rhythm of your day.



\### The Millisecond Pause



Ad-hoc, in-the-moment use. Something just happened. Someone said something. You are about to lose it. You open the app. One tap. Sixty seconds. No mode selection, no duration picker — just the state that worked for you last time.



This is the practice as reflex. You do it before you even know you need to.



\### The Closing Ritual



Every evening. Before sleep. The darkest version of the app. No chime at the end. No notifications. No social features. The phone dims. The session starts. If you fall asleep during it, that is the correct outcome. Chronicle logs it as \*Evening Release.\*



You said it yourself: \*I come home, I take a shower, I soak my feet in warm water, I prep something to eat, and I sleep.\* Catatonica lives right there, in that window before sleep. It is the final ritual of being alive for another 24 hours. You accept something, or you work on an idea, or you just lay there accepting the silence. Even after a tragedy.



This is the practice as liturgy. A ritual of closing.

\## What the Product Looks Like



The landing page, the onboarding, the dashboard — those surfaces use the ambient void field. Cold teal particles drifting slowly, warming subtly with time, responding faintly to where you move. Motion that invites. Atmosphere that introduces.



\*\*But the session itself is nearly empty.\*\*



When you enter a session, the ambient fades away. The visuals stop asking for your attention. Breath has one slow circle. Anchor has nothing — just sound. Silence after the hit has a screen so dark it is barely on, maybe one distant point of light to confirm the app is still with you.



The visuals are not the practice. \*The practice is the practice.\*



Cool animations belong to the frame — the space you step into and out of. Inside the session, the screen gets out of the way, because you are not meant to be watching. You are meant to be sitting. You are meant to be breathing. Even if you do not come up with anything, your system recognizes that it is safe to be still. To let life be itself without you constantly micromanaging and trying to control it.



No amount of beautiful visuals can change what you have to sit through. So during that sitting, the app becomes almost nothing. And that \*almost nothing\* is the gift.

\## The Voice, Later



Touch comes first. Solid, reliable, everyone can use it.



Voice comes after. For the man on the bicycle who cannot tap a screen. For the person doing dishes. For the one lying in bed in darkness who should not have to reach for the phone. Voice becomes a feature that grows alongside the product, adapts to the user over time, and can always be turned off. It is an invitation, never a requirement.



The app will eventually speak back. Softly. Only when asked. It reads what you entered back to you if you want it to. It confirms your choices. It tells you when a session begins and when you have stayed long enough. Then it goes quiet again.



The voice is not a personality. It is a companion's voice — the kind that does not need to fill the silence.

\## What Catatonica Will Never Be



No leaderboards. No streaks that shame you. No public profiles. No "today's mood" prompts that turn your inner life into data. No AI therapist pretending to understand you. No integration with productivity dashboards. No Apple Watch haptics that buzz when your breathing is "wrong."



Catatonica is not trying to optimize you. It is trying to give you back to yourself.



If a feature would make the product \*more engaging,\* that is usually a reason not to build it. Engagement is what your phone already does to you. This is the place you go to remember you are not a product.

\## The Vocabulary



These words belong to Catatonica. Using them consistently is what makes the product recognizable.



\*\*A Cataton.\*\* One minute of earned stillness. The smallest measurable unit of the practice.

\*\*A Situation.\*\* What you are carrying. Named, not solved.

\*\*The Void, The Threshold, The Ignition.\*\* Internal system names for the three states. Cosmology. Not shown to users in most contexts.

\*\*Breath, Anchor, Silence after the hit.\*\* User-facing names for the three states. Plain English.

\*\*The Waking Practice, The Millisecond Pause, The Closing Ritual.\*\* The three use contexts.

\*\*Planned Obsolescence.\*\* The release ritual. When a situation completes its lifespan in your life.

\*\*The Chronicle.\*\* The self-written journal of your practice.

\*\*The Order of the Still Voice.\*\* The name of the community of people who use this. Optional. Internal.

\*\*The Millisecond.\*\* The pause between punches. The whole thesis of the product in one word.



Every time we speak in our own vocabulary instead of industry-standard wellness-app language, we reinforce what Catatonica actually is. Every time we call a session a "meditation" or a ritual a "reset," we lose ground.
